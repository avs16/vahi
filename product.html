Description Details Storage Info

In Cakes: Sprinkle white chocolate chips on top of cake batter before baking, or fold them into the batter for a marbled effect.

In Brownies: Scatter white chocolate chips over the brownie batter before baking for a gooey, melted topping.

In Muffins: Mix white chocolate chips into muffin batter for a creamy surprise in every bite.

In Pancakes: Sprinkle white chocolate chips onto pancakes as they cook on the griddle, allowing them to melt slightly.

The packaging will list the ingredients used to make the white chocolate chips. Typically, they include sugar, cocoa butter, milk solids, vanilla, and sometimes lecithin as an emulsifier.

Cool and Dry Place: Store white chocolate chips in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Exposure to heat can cause them to melt and then harden again, affecting their texture.

Airtight Container: Transfer the white chocolate chips to an airtight container or resealable bag to prevent moisture from getting in. Moisture can cause the chocolate to seize or become grainy.

Room Temperature Softening: If your white chocolate chips become too hard due to cold storage, let them come to room temperature gradually before using them.

Avoid Water Contact: Keep white chocolate chips away from water or any liquids, as even a small amount of water can cause the chocolate to seize.
